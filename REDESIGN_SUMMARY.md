## Color Palette (Now Warm & Inviting)

Instead of bright teals and cool blues:

**New Palette:**
- **Warm Taupe/Brown** (#8B7355) - Primary color, grounded and earthy
- **Warm Gold/Tan** (#D4A574) - Accents, approachable and welcoming  
- **Soft Blue** (#7BA3BF) - Calm, trustworthy, warm (not clinical)
- **Warm Off-Whites** (#FAF7F2, #F5F0EA) - Backgrounds that feel like a warm embrace

**Why this works:**
These colors match Maria's existing brand (from her current site). They feel warm, human, and inviting—like someone actually cares about you, not just converting you.

---

## Typography (Elegant & Warm)

**Headings: Lora (Serif)**
- Elegant, literary, warm
- Says "I'm thoughtful and sophisticated"
- Creates an emotional connection
- Much warmer than sans-serif

**Body: Poppins (Sans-Serif)**
- Friendly, modern, readable
- Accessible and warm
- Conversational tone
- Not cold or corporate

**Together:** They create sophistication + warmth. You feel like you're having a conversation with someone refined, but genuinely caring.

## The Emotional Narrative (The Real Magic)

### Hero Section
**Old:** "You spent 15+ years perfecting clinical care."
**New:** "I see you. And I know exactly where you are."

This immediately makes the visitor feel *seen*. Not just acknowledged—truly seen. Maria knows them.

### Understanding Section
**Not just "Where you are."** It's "I understand what you're feeling."

The cards list the actual doubts and questions they have, not features or benefits:
- "Am I actually qualified?"
- "Will I be taken seriously?"
- "How much will this cost?"
- "Where do I even start?"

This is vulnerability. This is empathy. This says: "I know the real questions keeping you up at night, because I had them too."

### My Story Section
**Not credentials.** Maria's journey.

Instead of listing achievements, the site shares her vulnerability:
- "I went back to school"
- "I spent thousands"
- "I had to prove myself constantly"
- "No one should have to do what I did"

This builds trust through shared struggle, not through superiority.

### Transformation Section
**Not features or services.** Emotional outcomes.

What actually changes in your life:
1. "You stop feeling lost" (not "clarity on options")
2. "You become genuinely confident" (not "AI skills")
3. "Doors actually open" (not "job connections")
4. "You reclaim your narrative" (not "resume conversion")

Each one speaks to emotional transformation, not skill acquisition.

### Final CTA
**"You don't have to figure this out alone"**

Not a sales pitch. An invitation to not be alone anymore.

## Typography Choices

Notice the language throughout:

- **"I see you"** → not "Hello" or "Welcome"
- **"I've been exactly where you are"** → not "We serve hygienists"
- **"These aren't just guesses"** → acknowledges their skepticism
- **"No strings attached"** → removes pressure
- **"I'm with you every step"** → commitment, not transaction

Every word is chosen to make someone feel *known* and *cared for*.

## Design Details

### Warm Shadows
Instead of harsh black shadows:
```css
--shadow-md: 0 8px 24px rgba(139, 115, 85, 0.1);
```
The shadows are tinted with the warm brown color, creating a gentle, warm aesthetic.

### Subtle Accents
The top border on situation cards uses a gradient:
```
linear-gradient(90deg, var(--accent-warm) 0%, var(--soft-blue) 100%)
```
Warm to soft blue—warm to calm. It shows the journey.

### Breathing Room
Generous spacing throughout. It doesn't feel cramped or salesy. It feels like someone's taking time to really talk to you.

### Elegant Interactions
Buttons don't flash or attack. They gently lift. Shadows deepen. Transitions are smooth and warm, not sharp and aggressive.
